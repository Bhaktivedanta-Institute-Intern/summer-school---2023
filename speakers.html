Speakers

Prof. Earl K. Miller

Massachusetts Institute of Technology, USA

Earl K. Miller is the Picower Professor of Neuroscience with the Picower Institute for Learning and Memory and the Department of Brain and Cognitive Sciences at Massachusetts Institute of Technology, USA.

In 2020, Earl Miller was awarded an honorary doctorate (Doctor of Science, honoris causa) from Kent State University.

Earl Miller received a Bachelor of Arts degree in psychology from Kent State University in 1985, Master of Arts degree in psychology and neuroscience from Princeton University in 1987, and a PhD in psychology and neuroscience from Princeton University in 1990 under the supervision of the late Professor Charles G. Gross. He was a post-doctoral fellow in the laboratory of neuropsychology at the National Institute of Mental Health under the supervision of Robert Desimone from 1990 to 1995. In 1995, Earl Miller joined the faculty of the Department of Brain and Cognitive Sciences at MIT as an Assistant Professor of Neuroscience.

His research is based on studying neural mechanisms of attention, learning, and memory needed for voluntary goal-oriented behavior. His work also explores prefrontal function by employing various techniques including multiple-electrode neurophysiology, psychophysics, pharmacological manipulations, and computational techniques.

Professor Miller is the recipient of a variety of awards and serves as editor, and on the editorial boards of major journals in neuroscience, and on international advisory boards. His paper with Jonathan Cohen, (Miller and Cohen, 2001), which presented a new framework for understanding the prefrontal cortex, ranks fifth all-time in citations in neuroscience. In 2020, Earl Miller was awarded an honorary degree from Kent State University. He was named in the top 2% of scientists worldwide

Prof. Mayank Mehta

University of California, Los Angeles, USA

Prof. Mayank Mehta is Professor at University of California, Los Angeles. He is affiliated to the Department of Physics and Astronomy, Neurology, Neurobiology.

The mind is believed to be an emergent property of the actions of ensembles of neurons in his novel approach to memory studies. It is uncertain what these emergent features are and how they develop. His research focuses on this. One of the areas in which his team works to learn more is the method by which ensembles of neurons encode information about the physical world. Understanding how the brain perceives space-time and how these neural representations change as we learn is necessary for this. His group investigates how sleep affects learning using cutting-edge methods. He focuses on creating the next generation of artificial intelligence (AI) that is based on the human brain and non-invasive, reversible ways to turn on/off specific brain regions. In addition, he studies how individual neurons respond to virtual reality and how information is processed by neurons as they sleep to generate long-lasting memories. His findings would not only offer a fundamental knowledge of the dynamics of neuronal ensembles, but they would also suggest brand-new approaches to treating learning and memory problems. In order to comprehend how animals perceive space, he employs virtual reality. The theories put forth by Prof. Mayank Mehta demonstrate how virtual reality might enhance healthy brain rhythms by changing neuronal dynamics, circuitry, and plasticity.

His outstanding list of academic publications shows his command of the subject.

Prof. Neil Burgess

University College London, UK

Neil Burgess was born in 1966. He is a Professor of Cognitive Neuroscience at University College London and Principal Research Fellow for the Wellcome Trust. He has developed computational models that link behaviour to activity in biological brain networks, making significant contributions to our knowledge of memory and spatial cognition.

Burgess received his education at University College London (UCL), where he studied mathematics and physics as an undergraduate. He finished his doctoral education in theoretical physics at the University of Manchester's School of Physics and Astronomy under the direction of Michael A. Moore, where he started collaborating with Graham Hitch on memory model research and received a Doctorate in 1990.

Neuroscience studies by Burgess have created models that describe how neural networks enable us to represent, recall, and visualise our location within the surrounding environment. These models offer a mathematical insight of how human brain activity affects the function (and malfunction) of spatial memory, episodic memory, and autobiographical memory. Along with Colin Lever and Tom Hartley of Durham University and the University of York, he both anticipated and discovered neurons that encode environmental boundaries.

Burgess held a Royal Society University Research Fellowship prior to being elected a Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS) in 2017 and a Member of the Academy of Medical Sciences (FMedSci) in 2009

Prof. Neeraj Jain

IIT Jodhpur, India (former Director, National Brain Research Center, Manesar, India)

Dr. Neeraj Jain is a Professor in the Department of Bioscience and Bioengineering at IIT Jodhpur.He received his Ph.D. in 1986 from Indian Agricultural Research Institute, New Delhi. After working as Scientist at the Centre for Cellular and Molecular Biology, Hyderabad for six years, he moved to the Department of Psychology at Vanderbilt University, Nashville, USA as a Post-Doc. On return to India, he joined the newly established National Brain Research Centre at Manesar, near New Delhi in 2002. He was appointed Director-in-Charge of NBRC in 2017, and Director in 2018 before joining IIT Jodhpur in 2021.

His research interests lie in neuroscience/mammalian sensory and motor systems, tactile information processing, brain plasticity, spinal cord injuries, brain-computer interface, and brain networks. Current research interest is in determining how brains reorganize following injuries to the spinal cord and its perceptual and behavioral consequences.

Dr. Jain received a gold medal (1980) for securing first rank at Panjab University, Chandigarh in B.Sc. Microbiology; Young Scientist Award (1995) from John F. Kennedy Centre, Vanderbilt University, Nashville, USA; Fellow of the John F Kennedy Center for Research on Human Development Vanderbilt University, Nashville, USA (2001); International Senior Research Fellowship (2003-2008) from the Wellcome Trust, UK. He was Elected Member of Guha Research Conference (2016) and a Fellow of the National Academy of Sciences, India (2016).

Prof. Jayasri Das Sarma

IISER Kolkata, India

Dr. Jayasri Das Sarma is a Professor in the Department of Biological Sciences at IISER Kolkata, India.

She received her Ph.D. in 1995 from Indian Statistical Insitute (Jadavpur) in collaboration with the Indian Institute of Chemical Biology. After her brief stay at IISc Banglore as a Research Associate, she moved to the University of Pennsylvania, Philadelphia, USA in 1997 for her post-doctoral studies and continued her work till 2003. She was then appointed as an Assistant Professor (in 2004) at the Department of Neurology, Thomas Jefferson University, Philadelphia, and in 2008 she returned to India and became an associate professor at IISER Kolkata, India. Between 2012 to 2018, she held adjunct faculty positions at the Department of Pathology, Anatomy and Cell Biology and the Department of Ophthalmology at the University of Pennsylvania.

Her research work lies in Neurovirology, Neuroimmunology, and Neurobiology of disease mainly focused on murine coronavirus-induced neuroinflammation and chronic progressive myelin loss which are the pathological hallmarks of human neurological disorders like Multiple Sclerosis. Her other research interests include cervical cancer and ovarian cancer.

Prof. Jayasri has been the Elected Fellow of the Indian Academy of Neuroscience (IAN), Indian Academy of Science (IAS), and National Academy of Science, India (NASI) in 2021. She played the role of an Indian Host Mentor for the ASM-IUSSTF Indo-US International Professorship Award in 2018. She received ASMCUE Leadership Award for International Educators from the American Society for Microbiology (ASM) in 2017 and 2016. She received ASM-IUSSTF Indo-US International Professorship Award to develop a bilateral research relationship between India and the US in 2013.

Dr. Siddharth Warrier

Wockhardt Super Specialty Hospital, Mumbai, India

Dr. Siddharth Warrier is an experienced Neurologist. He obtained his MD from Seth GS and KEM Hospital in Bombay after completing his medical studies at Mumbai's Grant Medical College. In the SGPGI Hospital in Lucknow, he completed his DM Neurology programme. He also earned his MRCP UK certification from the University of London.

From 2019 to 2020, he served as a Senior Registrar in the KEM Hospital's Neurology department. In 2019, he obtained his MRCP UK (London) degree after completing his MRCP course. From February 2020, he has been a part of Wockhardt Hospital, Mira Road. The Maharashtra Society of Neurology and the Indian Association of Neurology both recognise Dr. Warrier as a Life Member. He is a member of the London-based Royal College of Physicians (MRCP). He also belongs to the European Academy of Neurology as a corresponding member.

He destigmatizes mental illness on his YouTube channel, where he also promotes dialogues about behaviour change, mindfulness, and learning. He also raises awareness about science, mental health, and human behaviour.

Prof. Sandeep Kumar

IIT(BHU) Varanasi, India

Dr. Sandeep Kumar is a Professor, Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT (BHU), Varanasi. He graduated from MNREC Allahabad and completed post-graduation from IT, BHU, Varanasi. His field of interest is Computational Mechanics. After completing Ph.D. from IIT Delhi in the field of composite plates and shells, he has worked in various fields of research such as meshless methods, chaos theory, and wavelets etc. Before joining IIT (BHU), he worked in REC Kurukshetra, BITS Pilani and AIMST, Malaysia. He has completed several research projects for DST and BARC. He has numerous publications in international journals of repute. He has written a popular book, “Mathematical Theory of Subdivisions - Finite Elements and Wavelet Methods”, which is published by CRC press.

Prof. Balaji Jayprakash

IISc Bangalore, India

Dr. Balaji is currently an Associate Professor at the Centre for Neuroscience, Indian Institute of Science.

He obtained his B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees in chemistry from Bharathidasan University, Trichy, India. He received his Ph.D. in 2004 from the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research, Mumbai. He then moved to the Department of Biochemistry, Weil Medical College of Cornell University, New York, in 2005 as a Post Doctoral Fellow. He then joined the Department of Neurobiology, UCLA, Los Angeles, in 2007 as a Post Doctoral Fellow under the supervision of Prof. Silva before joining the Center for Neuroscience at the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore, India in 2011.

His research interests lie in the area of learning and memory mainly focusing on understanding and establishing neuronal correlates of remote memory and its role in creative problem-solving by employing techniques such as optogenetics, CLARITY, Virtual Reality, and in-vivo imaging.

Dr. Srikanth Chandragiri

Max Planck Institute, Germany

Dr. Chandragiri Srikanth is currently working as a Ph.D. student at the Max Planck Institute for Biology of Ageing in Cologne, Germany.

He also worked at UNIFR Switzerland on the molecular mechanism of membrane contact site (MCS) formation and its role in facilitating the transport of biomolecules between the organelles.

Sushant Sharma

Bhaktivedanta Institute Kolkata, India

Sushant Sharma is a B. Tech. graduate (2000) from IIT Guwahati. He has contributed enormously towards various activities of the Bhaktivedanta Institute as an author and an editor. He was last associated with Tech Mahindra Limited as Tech Leader and has an IT experience of over 15 years. Later, Sushant Sharma decided to dedicate himself to the mission of Bhaktivedanta Institute and has been engaging himself to study the science of consciousness from a scientific as well as spiritual perspective. Under the able guidance of science-spirituality synthesis visionary Dr. T. D. Singh, he has delivered a key note lecture at the International Conference on Life and its Origin at Rome in 2004. From 2007, Sushant has been a speaker in many science-spirituality conferences and workshops organized by the Bhaktivedanta Institute including the AISSQ conference series. His conference lecture themes include “Man and Machine”, “Computer and Intelligence” and “Inimitable Intelligence”

Shri K Vasudev Rao

President, Bhaktivedanta Institute Kolkata, India

Vasudeva Rao (also known as His Holiness Bhaktisvarupa Vrajapati Swami), an alumnus of prestigious institute, IIT Kanpur (Computer Science, 1998), is currently the President of the Bhaktivedanta Institute, Kolkata. He is also the Editor of Bhaktivedanta Institute’s reputed annual journal, Savijnanam – Scientific Exploration for a Spiritual Paradigm and travels widely across India and abroad. His deep interest in the Foundations of mathematics, fundamentals of computer science and Logic and its relation to nature of reality as well as ancient Indian texts led him to interact and meet renowned scholars at Harvard, Princeton, Stanford, and MIT. He has delivered numerous talks on computer science and spirituality. Under his guidance and supervision, more than 10 national conferences, 2 International conferences, over 200 papers and over 100 seminars and workshops on science and spirituality have been organized by the Institute in past decade which includes contributions from more than 20 Nobel Laureates and world-renowned scholars.
