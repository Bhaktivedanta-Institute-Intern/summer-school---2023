Unfolding the Mystery of Memory

Every learning relies on memory since it allows one to store and recall the knowledge when required. Thus, memory serves as a repository for information that has been passively or actively retained. Even, we wouldn't have a feeling of identity or a sense of who we are without our memories. Since past several decades, serious researchers from different disciplines make memory study inherently cross-disciplinary. It is not merely a chemico-physical process of obtaining and reproducing electrical pulses that read events and information from the past. Instead, there is also a psychological process involved in operation for collecting, storing, and remembering the previous experiences. In addition to acting as a repository of past events, it can also act as a catalyst for emotional and cognitive variables that commonly simulate future events. Certain illnesses or diseases may negatively impact other bodily functions, a variety of diseased disorders can also impair memory. Memory loss is a common sign of several diseases, including dementia. Therefore, the study of memory is a very important topic for us in our existence on all levels, whether they be physiological or psychological
